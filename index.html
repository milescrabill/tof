Until now, you couldn't shoot video with automatic focus for under $1000

With HyperFocus, you can move your camera and leave the focus to us.

HyperFocus locates the objects your camera is pointing at and adjusts your lens to focus on them. Our prototype uses ultrasonic waves, but future versions will use LiDAR.

Reinvision the zoom shot

The days of manual are behind us. Focus on the future with HyperFocus. Move your camera with the world, and we will move the world with your camera.

The closest thing to this technology in the consumer market is Lenzhound, a $395 Kickstarted wirelessly controlled manual focus adjuster. The difference? We have ranging automatic focus, and can sell for less.

Powered by open source hardware

We use the Arduino Uno R3 microcontroller to register the ultrasonic sensor data and operate the stepper motor that adjusts the lens focus.

HyperFocus is open source, and powered by open source software.
